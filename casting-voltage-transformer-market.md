<p><p>A casting voltage transformer (CVT) is a type of instrument transformer used to step down high voltage to a lower, measurable voltage suitable for use in electronic measuring instruments, protective relays, and control systems. It consists of a high voltage primary winding and a low voltage secondary winding, encased in a cast resin material for insulation and protection.</p><p>The future of the casting voltage transformer market looks promising, with a projected growth at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 6.9% during the forecasted period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. CVTs play a crucial role in these systems by providing accurate measurement and monitoring functions.</p><p>Furthermore, the rising investments in renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power are expected to boost the demand for CVTs. These transformers are essential in integrating renewable energy into the grid and ensuring efficient power flow management.</p><p>Moreover, the ongoing advancements in technology, such as the development of smart grids and digital substations, are creating opportunities for CVTs.
