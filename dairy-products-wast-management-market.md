<p><p>Dairy products waste management involves various methods, including aerobic digestion (composting), anaerobic digestion, incineration/combustion, and others. Aerobic digestion refers to the breakdown of organic materials by microorganisms in the presence of oxygen, often used in composting dairy waste. Anaerobic digestion involves the decomposition of organic matter by microorganisms in an oxygen-free environment, generating biogas for energy production. Incineration/combustion entails the burning of dairy waste to produce heat or electricity. Other methods may include options like landfilling, landfill gas recovery, or the use of waste-to-energy technologies.</p></p>
